This is a question for the seniors: Do you guys remember Pocketspace?!

Before I was even born or just old enough to use the internet, Pocketspace was apparently the go-to blogging service everywhere. And here I thought LogDaily was old already!

So, I decided to do my research on what it was like. Everyone tells us the internet was really different back then... and they're right! Unlike our experience here where we can barely customize our themes properly (everything is template ready unless you pay for a subscription. NO THANKS!) Pocketspace allowed for a bunch of CSS modifications, so you could do pretty much anything with your blog's appearance... you just needed to learn a bit of coding really!

Not only that, the web environment was... different. I don't know how to describe it as someone who didn't really live that era, but everything felt so... cozy? Is that the right word? I guess the customizability of it all, and how the blog archives seem like little personal corners of the web you can just... access. It's weird. I like it.

Sadly, the Pocketspace servers got shut down back in 2014... Something about "failing to keep up with the market" and the team behind the service "shifting their focus". I hate to think about what it would be like to migrate from there... to be forced to leave a place you're so comfortable with.

I guess the future is all about sleek web pages and modern, flat logos... XP
